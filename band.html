Welcome, John Doe

James Hetfield

James Hetfield is the voice and rhythmic backbone of Metallica. Born on August 3, 1963, in Downey, California, Hetfield co-founded the band in 1981 alongside Lars Ulrich. Known for his powerful vocals and down-picking guitar technique, Hetfield has written some of Metallica's most iconic songs, such as "Enter Sandman," "Master of Puppets," and "One." His lyrics often explore themes of inner struggle, addiction, and resilience, reflecting his personal journey. Hetfield remains a driving force in Metallica's sound and vision.

Lars Ulrich

Lars Ulrich, Metallica’s co-founder and drummer, was born on December 26, 1963, in Gentofte, Denmark. A pioneer of thrash metal drumming, Ulrich's intricate rhythms and high-energy performances have been a cornerstone of Metallica's music since its inception. Aside from his musical talents, Ulrich is known for his business acumen and has played a key role in steering Metallica's career to global success. His contributions to songs like "Battery" and "One" showcase his versatility and creativity behind the drum kit.

Kirk Hammett

Kirk Hammett, Metallica's lead guitarist, joined the band in 1983, bringing his distinctive style and technical skill. Born on November 18, 1962, in San Francisco, California, Hammett’s solos blend melody, speed, and emotion, as heard in classics like "Fade to Black," "Creeping Death," and "The Unforgiven." A student of legendary guitarist Joe Satriani, Hammett incorporates a wide range of influences, from metal to blues, into his playing. His creative input continues to shape Metallica's evolving sound.

Robert Trujillo

Robert Trujillo has been Metallica’s bassist since 2003, adding his distinct energy and groove to the band’s sound. Born on October 23, 1964, in Santa Monica, California, Trujillo was previously a member of Suicidal Tendencies and Ozzy Osbourne’s band before joining Metallica. Known for his dynamic stage presence and technical prowess, Trujillo has contributed to albums like "Death Magnetic" and "Hardwired... to Self-Destruct." His innovative bass lines and deep connection with the music have solidified his place as a vital member of the band.
